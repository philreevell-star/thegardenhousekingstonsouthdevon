The route

Getting to Wonwell

  1. 1 Start at The Garden House and head into Kingston village — a five-minute walk. Pass the Dolphin Inn on your right, noting it for the return.
  2. 2 Follow the footpath signs through the fields out of Kingston, heading towards the coast. The path takes you through classic South Devon farmland with wide views opening up as you descend towards the estuary.
  3. 3 After approximately 50 minutes you arrive at Wonwell Beach on the Erme estuary. At low tide a large expanse of golden sand is revealed. The beach is dog-friendly year round and excellent for swimming when the tide is right.
  4. 4 At low tide you can also wade across the Erme to Mothecombe Beach on the far bank — check tide times before attempting the crossing.
  5. 5 Return the same way back to Kingston — or take the alternative route through Wrinkle Wood (see below). Either way, the Dolphin Inn is waiting.
Alternative return

Via Wrinkle Wood

From Wonwell Beach, follow the footpath inland along the estuary, then take the steep climb up through Wrinkle Wood. The path then crosses fields before joining a lane back into Kingston. This route adds variety and some lovely woodland walking but is considerably steeper than the outward path. A map or OS Maps app is recommended for this return option — download the route before you set out.

Start & parking

Start from The Garden House, Kingston TQ7 4PT. Walk five minutes into the village. Limited roadside parking in Kingston village near the church or Dolphin Inn.

Refreshments

The Dolphin Inn, Kingston — a traditional Devon pub five minutes from The Garden House, perfect for a drink or lunch on the return. Check opening times before setting out.

Terrain

Field footpaths and lanes. Can be muddy after rain — good footwear recommended. The alternative return via Wrinkle Wood involves a steep climb.

Tide note

Wonwell Beach is best at low tide when the sands are fully revealed. Check tide times before setting out — the BBC Tide Tables are a reliable free resource.

Be prepared

What to bring

Checklist for Walk 1

  • Waterproof walking boots or sturdy trainers — paths can be muddy
  • Waterproof layer — South Devon weather can change quickly
  • OS Maps app (downloaded offline) if taking the Wrinkle Wood return
  • Tide times checked in advance if planning to swim or cross to Mothecombe
  • Water and snacks for the beach
  • Dog lead for road sections and near livestock
  • Swimwear if the tide is right — Wonwell is a wonderful swimming spot
