The route

A circular coast and estuary walk

  1. 1 Start at The Garden House and walk five minutes into Kingston village. From the Dolphin Inn turn right, then left in front of Rock Cottage.
  2. 2 Follow the lane — signed as a no through road — then continue along the public bridleway at the end of the lane. At the gate, carry straight on along the permissive footpath signed to Westcombe Beach ½ mile.
  3. 3 Descend through the valley to Westcombe Beach — a wild, secluded cove with dramatic striped white and rust cliffs. No road access keeps this beach beautifully quiet. Take your time here.
  4. 4 From Westcombe, join the South West Coast Path and follow it steeply up onto the clifftops. The path hugs the coast with spectacular views along this stretch — one of the finest sections of the entire path.
  5. 5 The path curves around and drops down to Wonwell Beach on the Erme estuary. At low tide the sands are revealed and swimming is excellent. The views up the estuary to Mothecombe are stunning.
  6. 6 Return inland from Wonwell, following the cliff path towards Scobbiscombe Farm. Turn left at the sign and follow the fields as the path leads to a lane. Turn left to follow the road back into Kingston.
  7. 7 The Dolphin Inn is five minutes from The Garden House — a very well-earned stop at the end of the walk.
Easier return option

Avoiding the steep climb out of Westcombe

If you'd prefer to avoid the steep coast path climb up from Westcombe, there is an alternative return route past the waterworks which is less strenuous. Ask at The Garden House for details, or check the South Devon AONB walking guide for the Kingston circuit at southdevon-nl.org.uk.

Start & parking

Start from The Garden House, Kingston TQ7 4PT. Limited roadside parking in Kingston village near the church or Dolphin Inn. Alternatively park at Wonwell lane (grid ref SX 620477) and join the route there.

Refreshments

The Dolphin Inn, Kingston — a traditional pub dating from the sixteenth century, five minutes from The Garden House. Perfect for lunch or a drink on return.

Terrain

Bridleway, permissive footpath, South West Coast Path, field footpaths and surfaced road. Coastal sections can be exposed and steep. Can be muddy in wet weather. Good walking boots essential.

Navigation

The route is generally well signed but an OS Maps app is recommended — download the area offline before setting out. OS Explorer map OL20 covers this area.

Be prepared

What to bring

Checklist for Walk 2

  • Waterproof walking boots — essential on this route, especially in wet weather
  • Waterproof jacket and warm layer — exposed clifftop sections can be bracing
  • OS Maps app with the route downloaded offline, or OS Explorer OL20
  • Water and food — no refreshments between Kingston and the return
  • Tide times if planning to swim at Wonwell or cross to Mothecombe
  • Dog lead for road sections and near livestock — some fields have sheep
  • Walking poles useful on the steep coast path descent and ascent
  • Swimwear — Wonwell is a wonderful and rarely crowded swimming spot
  • Camera — the views from the clifftops are exceptional
