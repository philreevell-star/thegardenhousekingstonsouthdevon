Burgh Island
& Bigbury Beach

A classic South Devon day out — clifftop paths, Challaborough Bay, the wide sands of Bigbury Beach and the iconic Burgh Island, home to a 1930s Art Deco hotel and one of Devon's oldest pubs.

Check the tides before you set out

Burgh Island is accessible on foot across the sand at low tide. At high tide the crossing is by the famous Sea Tractor (£2 each way) — check times at the slipway on arrival before crossing to the island. A good tide app or the BBC Tide Tables will help you plan your timing.

Getting there

Ringmore is a 5-mile drive from Kingston

From The Garden House drive south towards Bigbury, following signs for Ringmore. The National Trust car park is at grid reference SX649456. Parking is free for NT members and £3 for non-members via the JustPark app — location code 80507. Download the app in advance as there is no cash or card payment on site.

The route

Clifftops, beaches and an island

  1. 1 From the car park, join the South West Coast Path heading towards the coast. The path leads through farmland with early views opening towards Bigbury Bay and Burgh Island on the horizon.
  2. 2 Follow the clifftop path south, with the sea to your left. The path descends to Challaborough Beach — a sheltered sandy bay. Follow the road briefly behind the beach before rejoining the coast path.
  3. 3 Continue along the coast path to Bigbury Beach — one of South Devon's largest and most popular sandy beaches. Good for a swim, a rest, or refreshments at the Venus Café in the lower car park (open daily, licensed, indoor and outdoor seating).
  4. 4 Cross to Burgh Island — on foot across the sand at low tide, or by Sea Tractor at high tide (£2 each way). Check the Sea Tractor times at the slipway before crossing so you know when it runs for your return.
  5. 5 On the island, climb to the summit for panoramic views across Bigbury Bay, Bantham and beyond. The small building at the top was once a huer's hut, where the lookout watched for pilchard shoals in the bay below.
  6. 6 Explore the island on foot. The Pilchard Inn is one of Devon's oldest pubs and an ideal stop — the bar dates from the fourteenth century and the views from the terrace are hard to beat. The Art Deco Burgh Island Hotel is also worth seeing from the outside.
  7. 7 Return to Bigbury by foot or Sea Tractor, then retrace the coast path to the Ringmore car park — allow approximately one hour for the return walk.

Parking

  • Ringmore NT car park (SX649456)
  • Free for National Trust members
  • £3 non-members via JustPark app
  • Location code: 80507
  • No cash or card on site — download JustPark in advance

Refreshments

  • Venus Café — Bigbury Beach, open daily, licensed, full menu including fish & chips, burgers and coffee
  • The Pilchard Inn — Burgh Island, 14th century pub, bar food and terrace with sea views

Sea Tractor

The Sea Tractor runs at high tide only and costs £2 each way. Check the timetable at the slipway on arrival. The crossing takes just a few minutes. At low tide the island is reached by walking across the sand — roughly 270 yards.

Toilets

Public toilets at Bigbury-on-Sea car park near the Venus Café. No facilities at the Ringmore car park or on the coast path.

Be prepared

What to bring

Checklist for Walk 3

  • Tide times checked in advance — plan your crossing to suit low tide if possible
  • JustPark app downloaded and loaded before leaving The Garden House
  • £2 per person for the Sea Tractor if crossing at high tide
  • Waterproof walking boots — clifftop paths can be muddy
  • Waterproof layer — exposed coastal walking whatever the season
  • Water and snacks — though Venus Café and the Pilchard Inn have you covered
  • Dog lead near livestock and on the beach sections (seasonal dog restrictions may apply at Bigbury)
  • Swimwear — Bigbury Beach is excellent for swimming
  • Camera — the views from the island summit are exceptional
  • OS Maps app useful for the return path to Ringmore
